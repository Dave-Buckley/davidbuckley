Consolidate the programme's output into job-application-ready materials and, if pursued, run a structured application campaign combining targeted applications, warm outreach, and interview preparation.

## Tasks

- [ ] Rewrite the CV for technical roles: summary, portfolio URL, pinned projects at top (JOB-01)

<details>
<summary>More info · JOB-01 · Technical CV</summary>

**What to do**
Produce a one-page CV optimised for technical-role ATS and human review. Top: name, location, `davidbuckley.vercel.app`, GitHub, LinkedIn. Then a three-line summary framed as an engineer. Then the five strongest portfolio projects with one-line outcomes. Education and prior roles below.

**What counts as complete**
PDF exported, uploaded to the portfolio (restores the `/cv.pdf` route if desired), referenced from LinkedIn Featured and GitHub profile README.
</details>

- [ ] Re-tune LinkedIn: headline, Featured section, Open to Work (JOB-02)

<details>
<summary>More info · JOB-02 · LinkedIn for job search</summary>

**What to do**
Update the LinkedIn profile for active job search. Headline becomes specific ("Junior Software / AI Engineer — shipping in public at davidbuckley.vercel.app"). Featured section updated with the three strongest projects. Open to Work enabled, visible to recruiters only. About section adjusted to lead with current skills rather than the transition narrative.

**What counts as complete**
Profile verified in an incognito window against a pre-defined checklist.
</details>

- [ ] Polish the GitHub profile README; pinned repositories match the portfolio (JOB-03)

<details>
<summary>More info · JOB-03 · GitHub profile</summary>

**What to do**
Create or rewrite the GitHub profile README (`Dave-Buckley/Dave-Buckley` repository, root `README.md`). Lead with the portfolio URL, two-line summary, three pinned repositories showing the strongest shipped work. Pinned repos order matches the Projects page on the portfolio.
</details>

- [ ] Publish a short "why I pivoted" narrative linked from the portfolio (JOB-04)

<details>
<summary>More info · JOB-04 · Pivot narrative</summary>

**What to do**
One long-form post explaining the reasons for the transition into engineering and what the portfolio demonstrates. Honest, professional tone. Linked from the About page and from LinkedIn. Serves as the answer when interviewers ask "why are you leaving teaching" so the conversation starts from the same baseline.
</details>

- [ ] Run an application pipeline — at least ten targeted applications per week via Otta, Wellfound, and direct approaches (JOB-05)

<details>
<summary>More info · JOB-05 · Application pipeline</summary>

**What to do**
Maintain a weekly cadence of at least ten substantive applications. Source: Otta, Wellfound, HN Who's Hiring, direct company careers pages. Each application includes a tailored short message and references the portfolio. Track applications in a private spreadsheet or database to enable follow-up.

**What counts as complete**
Sustained ten-per-week pipeline for at least eight weeks. Spreadsheet or database showing application status, dates, and outcomes.
</details>

- [ ] Run a warm-outreach pipeline — at least five warm contacts per week (JOB-06)

<details>
<summary>More info · JOB-06 · Warm outreach</summary>

**What to do**
Five personalised LinkedIn messages or emails per week to warm contacts — second-degree connections, engineers at target companies, referrals from the advocate network. Use the templates in `content/linkedin-templates.md`.

**What counts as complete**
Sustained five-per-week for at least eight weeks, tracked in the same system as JOB-05.
</details>

- [ ] Interview preparation — mock behavioural, mock technical, and at least three take-home exercises (JOB-07)

<details>
<summary>More info · JOB-07 · Interview prep</summary>

**What to do**
Complete at least three mock behavioural interviews (Pramp, Exponent free tier, or informal with the advocate contact). Complete at least three mock technical interviews covering DSA, system design basics, and an AI-specific technical discussion. Complete at least three take-home coding exercises published to the DSA repository or as separate projects.
</details>

- [ ] Landed role OR documented decision to pursue an MSc programme (JOB-08)

<details>
<summary>More info · JOB-08 · Outcome</summary>

**What to do**
Either: accept and sign an offer for a junior software or AI engineering role, OR: publish a clear, reasoned decision to pursue a specific named MSc programme with stated acceptance status. Ambiguous outcomes do not satisfy this task — one of the two must be documented.
</details>

- [ ] EU AI Act interview talking point prepared (JOB-09)

<details>
<summary>More info · JOB-09 · EU AI Act talking point</summary>

**What to do**
Prepare a short, articulate talking point on the EU AI Act — what it regulates, which system risk categories apply to which kinds of AI products, and what the implications are for engineering practice. Demonstrates awareness of the regulatory environment that UK and EU-adjacent roles will care about.

**What counts as complete**
A written one-pager on the portfolio (or a blog post) summarising the Act's key provisions for engineers, usable as interview prep.
</details>
